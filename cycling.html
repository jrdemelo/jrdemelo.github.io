Six years ago when I moved to Boston I was faced with a choice: I could spend 2 hours a day on the T getting to and from work or I could buy a cheap bike off Amazon and test my luck commuting the 3 miles to and from work. Needless to say choosing the bike was one of has been one of the best decisions of my life.

Over the past six years I've run through a half dozen bikes, racked up thousands of miles, started racing cyclocross, and have reached the point where I think competing in the Race Across America (RAAM) would actually be pretty fun. For the time being I'm focused on getting my USAC coaching license, chasing an eventual Cat 3 CX upgrade, and pretending I have any clue how to mountain bike.
